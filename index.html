What is "HAJIMI"?

A simple website about the origin, spread, and meme culture of Hajimi

Introduction

Hajimi (also written as “Hakimi” or “Hajimi”) is a popular internet meme. It did not originally refer to cats. Instead, it came from the anime Uma Musume: Pretty Derby. In fan-edited videos, a line spoken by the character Tokai Teio was remixed in a way that sounded like “Hajimi.”

Because the pronunciation sounded exaggerated and funny, many people began sharing it online. Over time, the sound clip was reused in many edits, memes, and remix videos. This made Hajimi recognizable to many internet users, especially in online video communities.

History of Hajimi

1. Origin

The origin of Hajimi can be traced back to fan culture around Uma Musume: Pretty Derby. Fans often edit scenes, audio clips, and music from anime into short videos. In one of these edits, the line from Tokai Teio sounded like “Hajimi,” which quickly caught attention because of how unusual and humorous it sounded.

2. Spread

Around 2023, the phrase became more widely shared online. It appeared in remix music, short edited videos, meme compilations, and parody content. As more people reused the sound, Hajimi became easier to recognize and spread to more communities.

  • Remix music
  • Edited videos
  • Internet memes
  • Short parody clips

3. Meme Culture

Over time, Hajimi developed into a recognizable meme rather than just a sound clip. It became part of online remix culture, where users repeat, transform, and repost media in funny ways. Hajimi is a good example of how a small edited moment from anime can become something much bigger through internet sharing.
